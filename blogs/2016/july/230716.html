Brutally Honest Reviews

about some apps I used in the last few days

So, I work at Beebom. It’s a super awesome website full of crazy cool tech resources. You got a problem? Chances are, Beebom has the solution. I’m not even kidding, we write articles about countless tech related issues, tips and tricks and stuff you didn’t even know you wanted, but you do. Show Us Some Love!

Anyway, working at Beebom means I get to use a shit load of apps, both on my Mac, and on my iPhone. So, a couple of days back, I was about to write an article about some eBook readers for the Mac, so I was reading through articles on Reddit, and going beyond page 1 of the Google search (LIKE WHAATTTT!!? But yeah, really!), and a lot of people had mentioned “Calibre” as being a really great eBook reader for the Mac.

Naturally, I assumed the app must be like the gold standard of the eBook apps available for the Mac, and I was… disappointed.

Actually no, I wasn’t disappointed. I was absolutely horrified. Jesus christ! Calibre is an app that I would never recommend to anyone. The only good thing about the app, and oh dear god, I wanted to find at least one good thing about it, was that it can convert eBooks to different formats. Pretty much an overkill on an eBook “reader”. The UI sucks, the UX sucks because the UI sucks, and the app sucks pretty much because the UI/UX sucks. I actually went and bashed Calibre on the Beebom post that I wrote, later that day, but I’m still so horrified by the thought of that app that I wanted to write about it here as well.

I’m not even being cruel here. I understand that it is difficult to code an app. Heck, I’m a developer myself. The thing is, one thing that Apple definitely makes easy for developers to do, is design the UI of their app. Honestly, Xcode is simply an amazing tool, and I could never have imagined something that looks as bad as Calibre does, to be coded on Xcode. In fact, it’s probably commendable that the developers were able to create a UI that bad; it must take a considerable amount of skill.

I’m so sure I had more apps that I wanted to talk about, but Calibre simply put them all out of my mind…
